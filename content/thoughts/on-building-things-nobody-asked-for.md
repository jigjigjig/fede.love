---
title: On building things nobody asked for
date: 2025-03
---

There's a particular kind of freedom in building something nobody asked for. No stakeholders, no sprint reviews, no "can we add one more thing before launch." Just you, a blank editor, and whatever idea has been rattling around your head at 2am.

Most of these projects go nowhere. They sit in private repos, half-finished, abandoned the moment the interesting problem was solved and all that remained was the boring glue work. And that's fine. The point was never to ship.

The point was to think by building.

Writing code to explore an idea is underrated. You can read about distributed systems all day, but writing a toy consensus algorithm — even a broken one — teaches you something no book can. Your hands learn what your eyes skip over.

I keep a folder of these projects. Some are embarrassing. A few surprised me by being useful. One became a tool I still use daily. But I didn't know that when I started it. I started it because I was curious.

If you're waiting for a good reason to build something, you've already missed the best reason: you wanted to.
